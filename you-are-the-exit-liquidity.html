Part VI

The Seven Ways They Take Your Money

There are exactly seven documented bot strategies extracting value from Polymarket. Each one has a different edge. All of them have the same victim: you.

Bot Strategy Taxonomy — Profit Ranges
Seven documented strategies, from $50K to $85M per operator

1. Information Arbitrage ($1M-$85M). Theo commissioned private polling. Our cluster uses real-time game APIs. They have information before you do. By the time you see the market move, they've already positioned.

2. Market Making ($1M-$10M). 147 bot wallets collect 61% of all trading fees — $11.1 million per day, roughly $75,000 per wallet. They post both sides of the book, earn the spread, and collect Polymarket's liquidity rewards (quadratic formula, ~$3M+/year budget).

3. Latency Trading ($100K-$500K). 0x8dxd turned $313 into $438,000 in one month by reading Binance WebSocket feeds and trading 15-minute Polymarket contracts before the market caught up. 98% win rate. Polymarket eventually killed this with dynamic taker fees.

4. Domain Specialization ($50K-$700K). gopfan2 made $700K+ trading weather markets by reading NOAA ensemble forecasts 5-10 minutes before human traders reacted. TeemuTeemu turned $900 into $230K trading esports using game API data that arrived 30-40 seconds ahead of Twitch streams.

5. NegRisk Rebalancing ($50K-$2M). When YES prices across a multi-outcome market don't sum to $1.00, bots buy all the underpriced NO tokens and merge for guaranteed profit. The arXiv research documented $39.6M extracted this way in one year alone.

6. Cross-Platform Arbitrage ($100K-$2M). Same event, different price on Polymarket vs Kalshi. Buy cheap, sell expensive. Free money if you can manage the execution risk across custodial and non-custodial platforms.

7. High-Probability Bonds (1,800% annualized). Buy outcomes trading at 95-99¢ near resolution. A bot buying at $0.95 with a 24-hour resolution earns 5.26% per trade — annualized at nearly 1,900%. The math is trivial. The capital efficiency is absurd.

Key takeaway

Every strategy has a common structure: bots compute an edge faster than humans can perceive it, execute at a latency humans can't match, and scale across more markets than humans can monitor. The human isn't providing a counterargument. They're providing liquidity.

Polymarket gives bots everything they need: transparent order books to analyze, zero identity barriers for multi-wallet operations, NegRisk frameworks that create structural arbitrage, and $0.007 transactions that make high-frequency strategies viable. This transparency is a double-edged sword. It lets researchers like me prove what's happening. It also lets bots see your orders, analyze your patterns, and trade against you with mathematically superior information.

Kalshi's KYC prevents sybil attacks. Its anonymous trades prevent counterparty profiling. Its rate limits constrain bot frequency. Its daily resolution windows limit latency arb. It's not perfect. But it's structurally harder for bots to dominate.

"Polymarket's on-chain transparency is a gift for researchers. It's a weapon for bots. And it's a trap for retail traders who think they're competing on a level playing field."

Methodology & Data

Dataset: 890M trades, 2.42M wallets, $50.87B volume (Nov 2022 — Mar 2026). Sourced from ClickHouse tables including polymarket_market_trades (~890M rows), leaderboard_by_pnl (2.42M wallets filtered to 819K with 50+ trades), polymarket_market_details (636K markets), and polymarket_market_prices (hourly + daily).

Bot Classification: 9-feature model (hourly entropy, maker ratio, log trades, trades/active day, trade size CV, weekend ratio, night ratio, market breadth, win rate) run through K-Means (k=4) + Isolation Forest (contamination=0.15) + composite weighted score (0-100). Thresholds: [0,30) Human, [30,50) Likely Human, [50,70) Likely Bot, [70,100] Bot.

Multi-wallet detection: Counterparty mapping, bilateral trade volume, co-trading analysis, temporal rotation patterns, first-trade analysis, and maker/taker role complementarity.

References: arXiv:2508.03474 ("Unravelling the Probabilistic Forest: Arbitrage in Prediction Markets"), arXiv:2603.03136 ("The Anatomy of Polymarket"), ChainCatcher on-chain analysis (95M transactions).
