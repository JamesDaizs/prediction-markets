The Numbers Don't Lie (But Polymarket Does)

I built a bot classifier using 9 behavioral features — hourly trade entropy, maker/taker ratios, trade frequency, size consistency, weekend activity, night trading, and market breadth — fed through K-Means clustering and Isolation Forest anomaly detection. I ran it across 38,839 wallets with 50+ trades.

The results are not subtle.

Part IV

The Bot Invasion Is Accelerating

In October 2025, there were approximately 98 wallets making 1,000+ trades per day on Polymarket. By March 2026, there were 2,506. That's a 33× increase in five months.

What happened? Open-source AI agent frameworks happened.

On November 24, 2025, Peter Steinberger created OpenClaw (originally Clawdbot). It's an AI agent framework that lets anyone deploy a trading bot on Polymarket. No quant degree needed. No HFT infrastructure. Just install, configure, and let the LLM figure out the trades.

On January 30, 2026, Polyclaw dropped — a Polymarket-specific skill for OpenClaw that handled all the CLOB plumbing. On February 9, Olas Polystrat launched an even simpler autonomous agent with a 10-minute setup. By February 16, Polymarket opened its US public APIs.

We're approaching a ceiling around 70%. Not because bots ran out of room, but because bots need human liquidity to extract from. You can't have a 100% bot market — there would be nothing left to take. The system stabilizes at the maximum extraction rate that still keeps enough humans coming back.

"The bot ecosystem stabilizes at ~70% volume share. Not because the bots ran out of strategies, but because they ran out of humans to eat. The predator-prey ratio has found its equilibrium."

Part VI

The Seven Ways They Take Your Money

There are exactly seven documented bot strategies extracting value from Polymarket. Each one has a different edge. All of them have the same victim: you.

1. Information Arbitrage ($1M-$85M). Theo commissioned private polling. Our cluster uses real-time game APIs. They have information before you do. By the time you see the market move, they've already positioned.

2. Market Making ($1M-$10M). 147 bot wallets collect 61% of all trading fees — $11.1 million per day, roughly $75,000 per wallet. They post both sides of the book, earn the spread, and collect Polymarket's liquidity rewards (quadratic formula, ~$3M+/year budget).

3. Latency Trading ($100K-$500K). 0x8dxd turned $313 into $438,000 in one month by reading Binance WebSocket feeds and trading 15-minute Polymarket contracts before the market caught up. 98% win rate. Polymarket eventually killed this with dynamic taker fees.

4. Domain Specialization ($50K-$700K). gopfan2 made $700K+ trading weather markets by reading NOAA ensemble forecasts 5-10 minutes before human traders reacted. TeemuTeemu turned $900 into $230K trading esports using game API data that arrived 30-40 seconds ahead of Twitch streams.

5. NegRisk Rebalancing ($50K-$2M). When YES prices across a multi-outcome market don't sum to $1.00, bots buy all the underpriced NO tokens and merge for guaranteed profit. The arXiv research documented $39.6M extracted this way in one year alone.

6. Cross-Platform Arbitrage ($100K-$2M). Same event priced differently on Polymarket vs Kalshi. Buy cheap, sell expensive. Free money if you can manage the execution risk.

7. High-Probability Bonds (1,800% annualized). Buy outcomes trading at 95-99¢ near resolution. A bot buying at $0.95 with a 24-hour resolution earns 5.26% per trade — annualized at nearly 1,900%.

Key takeaway

Every strategy has a common structure: bots compute an edge faster than humans can perceive it, execute at a latency humans can't match, and scale across more markets than humans can monitor. The human isn't providing a counterargument. They're providing liquidity.

Part VII

Methodology & Data

Dataset: 890M trades, 2.42M wallets, $50.87B volume (Nov 2022 — Mar 2026).
